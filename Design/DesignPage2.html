Style Guide

Goal and Aesthetic: I envision my portfolio website with a clean, neutral color scheme, using muted tones to keep users focused on the content without distraction. I aim for a modern, welcoming aesthetic with user-friendly navigation and clear, bold typography wiyh a clear theme. The site should maintain a professional look to appeal to potential employers and with a creative and unique style that grabs attention and reflects my personal approach. This phase focuses on defining the visual identity by choosing fonts, colors, and layout structures that align with the intended user experience.

1.1 Fonts – Readability & Legibility

Possible Choices:
  • Sans-serif fonts (e.g., Inter, Roboto, Montserrat) for a clean, modern look.
  • Headings: Montserrat Bold (for emphasis)(Havard website)
  • Serif fonts (e.g., Merriweather, Playfair Display) for a professional or elegant feel
  • Button text: All caps with medium, bold in white to grab user attention
Implemetation and fonts used:

The website’s typography is designed for both impact and readability. the Heading to create strong visual emphasis, reflecting a modern and professional tone inspired by the Harvard website. Body text chosen for its clean and highly legible style that ensures smooth reading. Button text is styled in all caps with a bold white font, helping calls-to-action and stand out.

1.2 Colour Palette & Colour swatches

Choices and elaboration:
  • Primary Palette: Different shades of blue for a sleek, professional interface and envoke a consistent theme
  • Secondary Palette: This is used to enhance the brand identity and bring visual contrast to the foundation set by the primary palette. It provides additional color options that can be used to highlight key elements or replace primary colors.

Through my research into possible colour palettes for the website, I found myself most drawn to shades of blue. The versatility and range within the blue spectrum offered a strong foundation for a recurring visual theme. Blue naturally reminded me of water, which sparked the idea of building the website around a Sea World concept. This association allowed me to explore different shades of the ocean to represent depth and calmness, while also opening up space for playful, creative touches. For example like using phrases such as "Dive into my work" to reflect the theme. This approach not only enhances the visual identity but also showcases a sense of creative wit and intentional design.

1.3 Composition – Hierarchy, Balance, Harmony

How elements are positioned to create a seamless experience.

Choices:
  • Grid-based Layouts: 3-4 column based pages to allow room for clickable images. Rows mays scroll down to the end of the Page(Blog Page).
  • Typography Hierarchy: Large, bold titles with smaller, readable body text.Paragraph always met with a heading,close to the margin. the only exception would be the home page, with more large headings for emphaysis
  • Layouts: The overall layout centers content for clear focus, with elements spreading outward toward the edges to create balance and flow. Paragraphs are arranged in a clean, column style format to create readability and structure. Clickable images follow this central-column layout as well, maintaining consistency across the site. However, the Portfolio page intentionally breaks this pattern to introduce variation and demonstrate versatility in layout design

1.4 Interface Elements – Visual Representations

Choice:
  • Buttons: Rounded edges for friendliness, for a mordern.
  • Icons: Minimalist, vector-based icons, next arrow will be the only one used for now,(Next page button)
  • Hover: square block with Rounded edges,blue shade
